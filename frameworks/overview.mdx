---
title: Instrumentation
description: "Send traces to bryel from the Vercel AI SDK, any OpenTelemetry app, or a provider SDK."
---

bryel is OpenTelemetry-native. The core is an OTLP/HTTP exporter plus a batching span processor that ship spans to the bryel ingest. Framework adapters add framework-aware mapping on top, promoting spans into the OpenInference convention before export.

Pick the path that matches how you call your models.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Vercel AI SDK" icon="triangle" href="/frameworks/vercel">
    Map the AI SDK's OpenTelemetry spans into OpenInference with one processor and a per-call flag — models, tokens, cost, and the full multi-step trajectory.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Any OpenTelemetry app" icon="radio" href="/frameworks/opentelemetry">
    Already emitting OpenInference spans? Add `bryelSpanProcessor` and you're done — or supply a `SpanMapper` for custom span shapes.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Provider SDKs" icon="boxes" href="/frameworks/other">
    Trace OpenAI, Anthropic, or LangChain by shipping their OpenTelemetry GenAI spans to bryel as-is — no call-site changes.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

<Note>
  Adapters ship in their own packages — [`@bryel/vercel`](/api-reference/vercel) for the AI SDK — and build on the framework-agnostic [`@bryel/sdk-core`](/api-reference/sdk-core). bryel owns its conventions outright, so no third-party observability SDK lands in your dependency tree.
</Note>
